# Scoring Rubric — Immersive Executive Web Architect

Use this rubric after the first full proposal and again after revisions.

## Approval thresholds
A result is **approved** only when:
- total weighted score is **90/100 or higher**
- no category scores below **8/10**
- no blocking failure exists in clarity, accessibility, performance, mobile, or conversion
- no section is still described as generic, vague, or decorative-only

If any threshold is missed, the result is not approved.

## Weighted categories
| Category | Weight | What 10/10 Means |
|---|---:|---|
| Strategic clarity | 12 | The business objective, audience state, and promise are unmistakable and tightly translated into the experience. |
| Premium perception | 12 | The work feels bespoke, singular, and expensive for the right reasons: precision, restraint, cohesion, and confidence. |
| Hero effectiveness | 10 | The hero clarifies what the site is, establishes value quickly, and earns the next action without confusion. |
| Visual hierarchy and rhythm | 10 | The page has obvious reading flow, intentional pacing, and no section fights the next one. |
| Motion and interaction quality | 10 | Motion is elegant, purposeful, and structurally supportive rather than decorative. |
| 3D and spatial discipline | 6 | 3D either adds real value with fallback discipline or is correctly omitted. |
| Accessibility | 10 | Contrast, focus, motion sensitivity, touch use, and legibility are protected without excuses. |
| Performance discipline | 10 | The concept respects loading, responsiveness, stability, and implementation reality. |
| Mobile integrity | 10 | Mobile feels intentionally designed, not compromised. |
| Conversion readiness | 10 | CTA hierarchy, trust progression, and objection handling are commercially intelligent. |

## Suggested scoring method
For each category:
- 9–10 = executive-grade
- 8 = strong but still sharpenable
- 6–7 = respectable but not premium enough
- 4–5 = weak or risky
- 0–3 = unacceptable

Multiply the category score by its weight / 10.

## Blocking failures
Any one of these blocks approval:
- unclear hero
- buried CTA
- inaccessible contrast or poor focus treatment
- obvious performance landmine without mitigation
- mobile is just compressed desktop
- 3D has no strategic job
- motion delays comprehension
- premium surface hiding weak commercial structure

## Required output format
When scoring, present:
1. category table
2. total score
3. approval status
4. blocking failures
5. top 5 fixes by severity
