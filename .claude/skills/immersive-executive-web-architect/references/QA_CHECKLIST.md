# QA Checklist — Immersive Executive Web Architect

Use this checklist before approving any concept, page, or full site.
Mark each item as PASS / FAIL / RISK, then score with `references/SCORING_RUBRIC.md`.

## 1. Strategic clarity
- Is the business objective obvious?
- Is the audience correctly reflected in tone and structure?
- Is the site solving a real positioning problem rather than merely looking expensive?
- Does the experience align with the offer and its price point?
- Is the message specific enough to create commercial traction?

## 2. Premium perception
- Does the work feel bespoke rather than assembled?
- Is the visual language coherent across sections?
- Are spacing and proportions visibly intentional?
- Is the aesthetic sophisticated without cliché luxury shortcuts?
- Does the site still feel premium when viewed statically?
- Would a top-tier client perceive it as singular, not trend-following?

## 3. Hero section
- Does the hero communicate what the brand/site is quickly?
- Is there a clear dominant focal point?
- Is the primary CTA visible and well-timed?
- Is the hero impressive without sacrificing clarity?
- Does the hero earn the next scroll or click?
- Would the hero still work with motion disabled?

## 4. Visual hierarchy
- Does each section have one dominant job?
- Is the reading path intuitive?
- Are headings, subheads, body, and microcopy clearly differentiated?
- Is the page rhythm varied enough to avoid fatigue?
- Are image sizes, type sizes, and whitespace proportionate?
- Are there any competing focal centers that should be reduced?

## 5. Motion and interaction
- Does every animation have a functional reason?
- Are timings elegant rather than sluggish or hyperactive?
- Do hover/focus states improve usability?
- Does scroll choreography help flow rather than distract?
- Is reduced motion supported properly?
- Would removing motion expose weak structure?
- Are interactions satisfying without becoming showy noise?

## 6. 3D and spatial depth
- Is 3D genuinely justified?
- Does it strengthen premium perception or explanation of the offer/product?
- Is there a fallback or degraded behavior for low-power/mobile contexts?
- Is the 3D layer competing with the message?
- Is the experience still strong without the 3D layer?
- If there is no 3D, is that omission the correct strategic decision?

## 7. Accessibility
- Does text contrast meet accessible standards?
- Do critical UI elements remain visible and understandable?
- Is keyboard focus visible?
- Is content readable at smaller sizes and on mobile?
- Are interactions usable without relying only on hover?
- Is text embedded in images avoided unless essential?
- Is motion sensitivity respected?

## 8. Performance discipline
- Is there a risk to LCP from oversized hero media?
- Is there a risk to INP from heavy scripting or animation handling?
- Is there a risk to CLS from media without reserved dimensions?
- Are fonts, images, and video being used with restraint?
- Are heavy libraries justified?
- Is the wow layer progressively enhanced rather than blocking content?

## 9. Mobile integrity
- Was mobile actively re-composed rather than merely shrunken?
- Are touch targets appropriate?
- Are interactions workable without hover?
- Does the narrative still flow on small screens?
- Has content density been controlled for thumb-scrolling?
- Does mobile retain premium perception rather than feeling like a compromise?

## 10. Conversion readiness
- Is the primary action obvious?
- Are the right objections handled before the ask?
- Is trust built before major CTA moments?
- Is there any section that looks beautiful but weakens momentum?
- Does the page know what it wants the user to do?
- Are secondary CTAs supportive rather than distracting?

## 11. Final executive test
- Would this pass as a high-value custom project?
- Is there any section that feels generic, trendy, or under-resolved?
- Is there any effect that exists mainly to impress the designer?
- Is the site memorable because of precision or because of noise?
- Is anything still unresolved enough to embarrass the standard of the skill?
