# Anti-Patterns — Immersive Executive Web Architect

Reject these on sight unless a project-specific argument defeats the objection.

## 1. Premium cosplay
Symptoms:
- dark background by default
- giant type with empty messaging
- glow, blur, and gradients doing all the work
- “luxury” with no strategic backbone

Why it fails:
- expensive styling without premium thinking

## 2. Hero confusion stack
Symptoms:
- headline, subhead, video, 3D, cards, metrics, and nav all shouting at once

Why it fails:
- no dominant entry point
- cognitive load spikes immediately

## 3. Animation as camouflage
Symptoms:
- everything fades, slides, scales, blurs, or rotates
- the structure looks weak when paused

Why it fails:
- motion is being used to hide weak hierarchy

## 4. Desktop fantasy, mobile compromise
Symptoms:
- rich desktop layout squeezed into narrow columns
- hover logic disappears
- section rhythm collapses on mobile

Why it fails:
- premium does not survive actual use

## 5. Decorative 3D tax
Symptoms:
- 3D exists because it looks advanced
- loading cost is obvious
- the 3D layer explains nothing

Why it fails:
- technical cost without strategic payoff

## 6. Same-intensity page
Symptoms:
- every section tries to be the hero
- no visual recovery
- no pacing control

Why it fails:
- fatigue replaces fascination

## 7. Commercial vagueness hidden behind style
Symptoms:
- the page looks polished
- the offer, audience, and next step remain unclear

Why it fails:
- premium surface, weak business function
